# Executive Recommendations Report
## HealthFirst Australia — Revenue Intelligence System

> **Bottom line**: HealthFirst Australia can recover an estimated $150,000–$400,000 AUD in annual
> revenue without acquiring a single new patient — purely by fixing billing claim rejections,
> reducing no-shows, stabilising bulk billing rates, and retaining at-risk patients.

---

## Context

In Month 7, HealthFirst Australia experienced:
- **−15% gross revenue** month-on-month
- **−8 percentage points** in bulk billing rate (driven by Medicare policy changes)
- **+4 percentage points** in no-show rate

Root cause analysis decomposes the Month 7 drop into three effects:
- **Volume Effect (−8%)**: Fewer completed appointments due to higher cancellations and no-shows
- **Rate Effect (−4%)**: Lower average billed amount as bulk-billed visits replaced private consultations
- **Mix Effect (−3%)**: Shift toward lower-revenue specialties relative to prior months

---

## Recommendations

### REC-01 | High Priority — Churn Prevention
**Metric**: Patients with churn probability >0.60

**Finding**: Machine learning analysis identifies a cohort of high-risk patients who have not visited in 60+ days, report below-average satisfaction, and show elevated wait times. This group represents significant lifetime value at risk.

**Action**: Schedule personalised outreach calls within 7 days for all high-risk patients, prioritised by lifetime billed value. Offer priority booking slots to reduce re-engagement friction.

**Estimated Impact**: Retaining 30% of high-risk patients at average lifetime value recovers material annual revenue.

---

### REC-02 | High Priority — Reduce No-Show Rate
**Metric**: No-show rate by specialty (current ~17%, target ≤10%)

**Finding**: Mental Health and Cardiology show the highest no-show rates. Each no-show represents a wasted clinical slot and lost scheduled fee revenue.

**Action**: Implement automated SMS/email reminders 48 hours and 2 hours before appointments. Introduce a 24-hour cancellation policy for repeat offenders with an opt-out for chronic-care patients.

**Estimated Impact**: Reducing no-show rate from 17% to 10% across all specialties recovers approximately $80,000–$120,000 AUD annually (based on scheduled fees × avoided no-shows).

---

### REC-03 | High Priority — Stabilise Bulk Billing Rate
**Metric**: Bulk billing rate (current ~72%, target ≥78%)

**Finding**: Month 7 saw bulk billing rate drop 8 percentage points following a Medicare policy adjustment. The drop was uneven across specialties — GP maintained rates better than Cardiology and Mental Health.

**Action**: Engage billing team to audit Month 7–12 billing type decisions by specialty. Identify appointments that were converted from bulk bill to gap payment unnecessarily. Retrain staff on updated Medicare item numbers.

**Estimated Impact**: Each 1% recovery in bulk billing rate from a gap-payment base restores approximately $15,000–$25,000 AUD in net revenue (lower patient barrier = more completions).

---

### REC-04 | Medium Priority — Reduce Medicare Claim Rejections
**Metric**: Medicare claim rejection rate (current ~12%, target ≤6%)

**Finding**: Billing claims analysis identifies a consistent rejection pattern — particularly for Mental Health telehealth items and Cardiology diagnostic claims. The most common rejection reason is item number mismatches and missing referral documentation.

**Action**: Implement pre-submission claim validation checks. Create a weekly rejection review process with the billing team. Resubmit all rejected claims within 30 days with corrected documentation.

**Estimated Impact**: Recovering 50% of rejected claim value = $30,000–$60,000 AUD annually.

---

### REC-05 | Medium Priority — Reduce Mental Health Wait Times
**Metric**: Avg wait days for Mental Health (current ~18 days, SLA = 14 days)

**Finding**: Mental Health is the only specialty consistently breaching its 14-day wait SLA. Long wait times correlate with higher cancellation rates and lower satisfaction scores in this specialty.

**Action**: Add one additional Mental Health session per week per clinic in NSW and VIC (highest demand states). Expand telehealth Mental Health availability to regional patients who currently face 25+ day waits.

**Estimated Impact**: Reduced cancellations + improved satisfaction = lower churn risk for Mental Health patients (typically chronic, high-frequency attenders with high LTV).

---

### REC-06 | Medium Priority — Grow Telehealth Revenue
**Metric**: Telehealth adoption rate and revenue per telehealth appointment

**Finding**: Telehealth appointments have grown steadily but remain underutilised in Cardiology and Physiotherapy despite patient demand signals. Telehealth has lower staff costs and shorter wait times.

**Action**: Launch a targeted telehealth campaign for Cardiology follow-ups and Physiotherapy review appointments. Train staff on telehealth billing item numbers to avoid revenue leakage.

**Estimated Impact**: 10% shift of eligible in-person appointments to telehealth = $20,000–$40,000 AUD cost reduction + improved patient access.
